Compassion Letter Lab

{# Intro — English, ≤ 200 words, neutral/inviting tone. Draft, pending review. #}

Every year, around 77,000 letters travel between sponsors and the children they support. Each one carries something personal — and each one deserves a careful check before it is sent, so that nothing unsafe or unclear slips through.

That volume is hard to review by hand, every letter, every time. So we are trying something: an AI assistant that drafts a translation and flags anything that might need a closer look.

It is not here to replace you. Your judgment — your feel for tone, names, and what a family actually means — is something the AI simply does not have. That is exactly why we are testing it with you, not instead of you.

In this lab you will read real letters and tell us where the AI gets it right and where it falls short. Your answers show us whether this kind of help can be trusted — and where it still needs a human. Thank you for lending your eye.

Common questions

What does this test?

Whether an AI assistant can translate sponsor–child letters well and reliably flag anything that needs a human’s attention. You are helping us see where it can be trusted and where it still cannot.

What happens to my responses?

They are kept only to measure how the AI performs — never shown to sponsors or children, and never used to judge you. We look at the overall results, not at individuals.

How long does it take?

As long as you like. Most letters take a minute or two, and you can stop any time — your progress is saved, so you can pick up again later.

Will the AI replace volunteers?

No. The aim is to support your work, not to remove it. Keeping human judgment in the loop is the whole point — that is exactly what we are checking the AI against.
